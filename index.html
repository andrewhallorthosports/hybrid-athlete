Results

Key findings

Results What the literature shows
FINDING 01
Injury risk in HIFT did not appear uniformly greater than in traditional training approaches.
FINDING 02
Risk was multifactorial, more commonly linked to chronic loading and overuse than to isolated events.
FINDING 03
Prevention was strongly influenced by coaching quality, load management, and movement competency.
FINDING 04
These modalities were associated with favourable cardiometabolic adaptations.
FINDING 05
Evidence suggested benefits for mental health and wellbeing, an area that remains under-studied.
FINDING 06
The literature was heterogeneous in design, populations, and terminology, limiting comparability.

Conclusion
The musculoskeletal risks identified are likely substantially outweighed by the wider physical, mental, and social benefits of exercise participation.
